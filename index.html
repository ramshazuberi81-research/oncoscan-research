Six pillars of scientific novelty

Every component of OncosenseAI addresses a specific gap in existing GI oncology AI. Here is what makes this architecture different from everything that already exists.

Classification Statement

OncosenseAI is not a diagnostic chatbot, risk calculator, or screening app. It is a Continuous Biological Auditing System — a silent operator that remains passive until the mathematics of patient data reaches a genuine bifurcation point requiring human clinical judgment.

Novelty 01
Neural ODE Temporal Engine
Standard AI models are blind to time between visits. OncosenseAI treats patient health as a continuous fluid state via Neural Ordinary Differential Equations — predicting biological derivatives, not snapshots.
Continuous dh/dt modelling Discrete visit snapshots
Novelty 02
Counterfactual Synthetic Twin
The AI builds a latent digital twin and runs thousands of bifurcation simulations — showing the patient not just what is happening, but the exact moment risk paths diverge and when intervention changes outcomes.

Novelty 03
3D Thermal Proprioception UI
Risk is visualised as heat on a patient-specific 3D anatomical model — where heat represents CMAT attention weight, not pain. A patient sees where the model's suspicion is focused before a physician speaks.
Attention-mapped anatomy Percentage score displays
Novelty 04
Zero-Notification Passive Monitoring
The system monitors digital biomarkers (gait, voice tremor, typing cadence) as proxies for systemic inflammation — asking no questions, generating no alerts, until the Neural ODE detects non-linear risk acceleration.
Silent operator model Daily questionnaire apps
Novelty 05
Cross-Ancestry SGRS Normalisation
The Surrogate Genetic Risk Score normalises across ethnic cohorts, directly addressing the data bias problem that renders most GI oncology models unreliable for non-European ancestry populations.
Zero-bias multi-ancestry European-dominant training
Novelty 06
Velocity-Aware Cross-Modal Attention
Attention weights are not just importance-weighted — they are volatility-weighted. The model is forced to prioritise the most rapidly-changing biological signal, not the highest absolute value.

The Problem

GI cancers (pancreatic, gastric, oesophageal) are diagnosed at Stage III-IV in 70–80% of cases. Five-year survival rates remain 10–20% for pancreatic cancer. Existing screening tools require symptomatic patients — by the time symptoms appear, the window for curative intervention has closed.

Current AI approaches treat each visit as an isolated snapshot, cannot handle irregular sampling, ignore genomic-symptom dissonance, and are trained predominantly on European ancestry cohorts. OncosenseAI addresses all four gaps simultaneously.

About the Project

Built for early detection

OncosenseAI is a research-grade clinical AI system designed to support early detection of gastrointestinal cancers — pancreatic, gastric, and oesophageal — where early-stage identification can multiply survival outcomes by nearly 9×.

The pipeline integrates patient symptom scoring, laboratory biomarkers, genomic profiling, and validated survival models into a cohesive 9-stage clinical decision support tool. Treatment recommendations are anchored to ESMO 2023, NCCN v2.2024, and NICE NG12/NG83 guidelines.

Validated retrospectively against 500 SEER patients across three cancer sites, with full TRIPOD reporting compliance and explainability through SHAP feature importance analysis. Phase 2 development targets Neural ODE temporal integration and cross-ancestry SGRS normalisation.

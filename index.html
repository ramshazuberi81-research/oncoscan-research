The pipeline

Four modules.
Every step traceable.

01
Symptom Intelligence Engine
17-variable weighted risk model. Patient demographics, alarm features, symptom duration, family history, prior GI diagnoses. Outputs stratified 12-month malignancy risk with key driver attribution.
Example output
Risk: 72% (High)
Drivers: age >50, abdominal pain,
family history, H. pylori
02
Visual AI Diagnostics
Simulated OpenCV image analysis pipeline for stool, urine, and abdominal imaging. Colour profiling, symmetry scoring, and occult blood signal detection. Research prototype — not validated imaging AI.
Example output
Stool: occult blood HIGH
Abdomen: soft-tissue density
variation detected
03
Treatment Matcher
Deterministic decision tree against NCCN/ESMO guidelines. Every branch is auditable. Incorporates HER2, PD-L1 CPS, MSI-H, KRAS, BRAF, BRCA. Real-time trial matching via ClinicalTrials.gov API.
Stage III · HER2+ · CPS=8
→ FLOT4 + D2 gastrectomy
→ Trastuzumab + CAPOX
→ Nivolumab (CPS≥5)
→ 5 matched trials
04
Clinical Report Generator
One command. Structured PDF output. Full pipeline summary including risk stratification, treatment protocol, IO eligibility, genomic flags, and matched trial list. Exportable research document.
Example output
PDF: PATIENT_001
Gastric Stage III · ECOG 1
Protocol: FLOT4 + D2
IO eligible: CPS 8 ≥ 5
Known limitations — plainly

Vagueness in clinical AI
is a form of dishonesty.

Until prospective validation exists, no clinical weight should be placed on outputs.

⚠
Guideline version
Pinned to NCCN v2.2024 / ESMO 2022. New approvals are not automatically reflected.
⚠
Missing markers
CLDN18.2, FGFR2b, and TMB markers are not yet encoded in the decision tree.
⚠
Patient complexity
No comorbidities, organ function, renal/hepatic impairment, or patient preference inputs.
⚠
Trial matching
Keyword-based matching only. Eligibility criteria are not verified against patient data.
⚠
No validation cohort
Not validated on real patient cohorts. IRB pilot (100-patient prospective) is the next step.
⚠
Rule-based brittleness
Encodes what guidelines say, not what past practice did. Novel presentations may not match any branch.

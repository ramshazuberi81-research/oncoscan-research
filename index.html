About This Stage

Stage 1 captures structured symptom data using NICE NG12 alarm feature criteria. The system automatically identifies red flag combinations — such as weight loss with jaundice and dark urine — that are pathognomonic for biliary obstruction and require the highest urgency pathway.

Patient-reported symptoms are weighted by SHAP-derived feature importance and fed into the ensemble model (Logistic Regression + Gradient Boosting + Random Forest) at Stage 4.

⚠ What Causes the Flag — NICE NG12 Alarm Features

Weight loss + jaundice + pale stools + dark urine together are NICE NG12 red flag symptoms. This constellation is pathognomonic for biliary obstruction — most commonly caused by a pancreatic head mass. Combined with back pain (suggesting retroperitoneal involvement) and 3 months of progressive symptoms, this triggers the highest urgency pathway. 4 out of 5 alarm features are present.

About the Project

Built for early detection

OncosenseAI is a research-grade clinical AI interface designed to support early detection of gastrointestinal cancers — pancreatic, gastric, and oesophageal — where early-stage identification can multiply survival outcomes by nearly 9×.

The pipeline integrates patient symptom scoring, laboratory biomarkers, genomic profiling, and validated survival models into a cohesive 8-stage clinical decision support tool. Treatment recommendations are anchored to ESMO 2023, NCCN v2.2024, and NICE NG12/NG83 guidelines.

Validated retrospectively against 500 SEER patients across three cancer sites, with full TRIPOD reporting compliance and explainability through SHAP feature importance analysis.
